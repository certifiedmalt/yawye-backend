Visual 1 — Chapter 3: The Refinery

Two Processes. One Difference.

Vegetable Oil Refining
1 Seeds crushed and pulverised into fine meal
2 Washed in hexane — a petroleum-derived solvent
3 Heated to evaporate the solvent
4 Degummed with acid or steam
5 Neutralised with caustic soda (NaOH)
6 Bleached with acid-activated clays
7 Deodorised at 200–260°C
Bottled as "vegetable oil"
Fuel Refining
1 Crude oil distilled and separated
2 Solvents and catalysts used to separate fractions
3 Heated to remove impurities
4 Treated with chemicals to remove residues
5 Neutralised to remove acids
6 Bleached or filtered to remove colour
7 Heated to high temperatures to stabilise
Stored as fuel

One is sold as food.
The other is sold as fuel.

Visual 3 — Chapter 5: The Fizzy Drink

Anatomy of a Fizzy Drink

1

Phosphoric Acid

Creates the signature "bite" in colas. Drops pH so low bacteria can't grow.

Also in: rust removers, industrial descalers
2

Citric Acid

Enhances fruitiness and boosts sweetness perception. Industrially produced by fermenting sugar with mould.

Also in: dishwasher tablets, limescale removers
3

Sweetness

9+ teaspoons of sugar per can, or high-intensity sweeteners hundreds of times sweeter. Triggers dopamine reward pathways.

4

Carbonation

CO2 triggers pain receptors in the mouth — the same ones activated by mustard and chilli. Disguised as "refreshment."

5

Caramel Colour

Industrial pigment created by heating sugars with acids or alkalis. Not caramel from a pan. Makes chemical water look like a treat.

6

Flavour System

Dozens of synthetic compounds — aldehydes, esters, aromatic chemicals — blended to create a sensory illusion. Proprietary. Engineered.

7

Preservatives

Sodium benzoate, potassium sorbate. Prevent microbial growth. Can form trace benzene under heat + light + vitamin C.

This is not a beverage. It is a chemical system.
