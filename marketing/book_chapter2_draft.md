## THE SYSTEM BEHIND THE SYSTEM

In the early 2000s, a team of researchers at the University of Sao Paulo in Brazil did something the food industry did not want done.

They stopped looking at food by nutrients — and started looking at food by *what had been done to it*.

Not "how much fat?" Not "how many calories?" Not "what percentage of your daily vitamin C?"

Instead, they asked a different question:

**How much has this been processed?**

It sounds simple. It changed everything.

Because when you stop measuring *what's in the food* and start measuring *what's been done to the food*, the entire modern diet reveals itself.

The result was the NOVA classification system — a four-group scale that categorises every edible product on Earth by its degree of processing.

Not by calories. Not by fat content. Not by sugar grams. Not by marketing claims.

By *what happened to it before it reached you*.

The World Health Organisation now uses this scale. Governments reference it. Researchers build studies around it. Public health bodies across the world have adopted it as the clearest lens for understanding the modern diet.

And the food industry has spent years trying to discredit it.

That alone should tell you how powerful it is.

## THE FOUR GROUPS

### GROUP 1 — UNPROCESSED OR MINIMALLY PROCESSED FOODS

These are foods that come from nature and remain close to their natural state.

They may be:
- washed
- peeled
- cut
- chilled
- frozen
- boiled
- roasted
- pasteurised

But they have not been fundamentally altered. They are still recognisable as the thing they came from.

**Examples:**
- Fresh fruit and vegetables
- Eggs
- Fresh meat and fish
- Milk
- Plain yoghurt
- Nuts and seeds
- Grains (rice, oats, wheat)
- Dried beans and lentils
- Herbs and spices
- Water

These foods are the foundation of human nutrition. They are what your body evolved to eat. They contain the fibre, protein, fats, vitamins, and minerals your biology needs — delivered in the form your biology recognises.

Group 1 is what food looks like before the system gets involved.

### GROUP 2 — PROCESSED CULINARY INGREDIENTS

These are substances extracted from Group 1 foods and used in cooking.

They are not eaten alone. They are tools — used to prepare, season, and cook real food.

**Examples:**
- Olive oil
- Butter
- Salt
- Sugar (in small quantities, as a seasoning)
- Honey
- Flour
- Vinegar

These ingredients have been processed — pressed, ground, refined — but they serve a clear purpose: to turn Group 1 foods into meals.

Group 2 is the kitchen toolkit. It has existed for thousands of years.

### GROUP 3 — PROCESSED FOODS

These are Group 1 foods that have been altered using Group 2 ingredients — typically to preserve them or make them more enjoyable.

The key distinction: **you can still identify the original food.**

**Examples:**
- Tinned vegetables (with salt)
- Cheese
- Freshly baked bread (flour, water, salt, yeast)
- Smoked fish
- Salted nuts
- Cured meats
- Pickled vegetables
- Jam (fruit + sugar)

These foods have been processed, but they are still recognisable. They still behave like food. They still interact with your biology in predictable ways.

Group 3 is traditional food preparation. It is what your grandmother would recognise.

### GROUP 4 — ULTRA-PROCESSED FOODS

This is where everything changes.

Group 4 products are not modified foods. They are **formulations** — industrial creations made mostly or entirely from substances derived from foods and additives.

They are designed to be:
- hyper-palatable
- convenient
- cheap to produce
- long-lasting
- highly profitable

They contain ingredients you would never find in a kitchen:
- emulsifiers
- stabilisers
- humectants
- flavour enhancers
- hydrogenated oils
- modified starches
- protein isolates
- high-fructose corn syrup
- artificial sweeteners
- colours
- preservatives
- anti-foaming agents
- bulking agents
- glazing agents

**Examples:**
- Fizzy drinks
- Energy drinks
- Crisps
- Biscuits
- Chocolate bars
- Ice cream (most commercial)
- Instant noodles
- Chicken nuggets
- Hot dogs
- Packaged bread (with emulsifiers and preservatives)
- Breakfast cereals
- Protein bars
- Flavoured yoghurts
- Ready meals
- Margarine
- Infant formula
- "Diet" and "zero" products
- Plant-based meat alternatives

The defining feature of Group 4 is not any single ingredient. It is the **purpose**:

These products are not designed to nourish. They are designed to **sell**.

They are engineered for:
- maximum shelf life
- minimum production cost
- maximum palatability
- maximum consumption frequency
- maximum profit

Group 4 is not food with additives. It is additives with food.

## THE LINE THAT MATTERS

The most important line in the entire food system is not between "healthy" and "unhealthy."

It is not between "organic" and "conventional."

It is not between "low fat" and "full fat."

The line that matters is between **Group 3 and Group 4**.

On one side: foods that have been processed but remain recognisable, traditional, and biologically familiar.

On the other side: industrial formulations designed in laboratories, optimised in factories, and sold through psychology.

This is the line the food industry does not want you to see.

Because once you see it, the entire supermarket reorganises itself in your mind.

The "healthy" cereal with 14 ingredients, three sweeteners, and an emulsifier? Group 4.

The "protein" bar with whey isolate, palm oil, maltitol syrup, and soy lecithin? Group 4.

The "zero sugar" fizzy drink with aspartame, acesulfame K, phosphoric acid, and caramel colour? Group 4.

The "natural" flavoured yoghurt with modified starch, gelatin, and artificial flavouring? Group 4.

The plain block of cheese in your fridge? Group 3.

The tin of sardines in olive oil? Group 3.

The bag of oats? Group 1.

The apple in your hand? Group 1.

The line is not complicated. But it is invisible — unless you know where to look.

Now you know where to look.

## WHY THE FOOD INDUSTRY HATES NOVA

The NOVA scale is dangerous to the food industry for one reason:

**It cannot be gamed.**

Calorie counts can be gamed. Serving sizes can be manipulated. Health claims can be engineered. Traffic-light labels can be designed around.

But NOVA asks one question that cannot be dodged:

*What has been done to this product?*

A product cannot hide its processing. It cannot disguise its emulsifiers. It cannot pretend its 27-ingredient formulation is "natural."

NOVA strips away the marketing and reveals the manufacturing.

This is why the food industry has funded campaigns to discredit it. This is why industry-linked nutritionists call it "too simplistic." This is why lobbyists push for alternative classification systems that focus on nutrients — systems they can manipulate.

Because NOVA doesn't measure what's on the label. It measures what's behind the label.

And behind the label is where the truth lives.

## THE NUMBERS THAT CHANGED THE CONVERSATION

When researchers started applying the NOVA scale to national diets, the results were staggering.

In the UK, more than **50% of household calories** now come from Group 4 ultra-processed foods.

In the US, that figure rises to **nearly 60%**.

For children and teenagers, the number is even higher — with some studies showing **up to 70% of their calories** coming from ultra-processed products.

These are not fringe findings. These are population-level data, published in peer-reviewed journals, replicated across countries, and consistent over time.

The modern diet is no longer dominated by food.

It is dominated by formulations.

And the consequences — as the following chapters will show — are written across the health of an entire civilisation.

## WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU

You don't need to memorise the NOVA scale. You don't need to carry a chart. You don't need to study food science.

You just need to ask one question:

**Could I make this in a kitchen?**

If the answer is yes — even roughly — it's probably Group 1, 2, or 3.

If the answer is no — if the product requires industrial machinery, chemical solvents, emulsifiers, stabilisers, and flavour systems to exist — it's Group 4.

That single question is more powerful than any calorie counter, any macro tracker, any health claim, any traffic-light label.

Because it cuts through the noise and reveals the truth:

**Is this food? Or is this a product?**
